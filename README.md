## üìñ The Story

We started with a simple premise: to answer the question, **"If you had Apple's data, what would you uncover?"**

To do this, we didn't just build a dashboard; we simulated a real-world enterprise analytics environment. We took raw Sales and Warranty tables and transformed them into a comprehensive product lifecycle monitor.

**It began with Architecture.**
Before drawing a single chart, we established a rigorous foundation. Led by our **Data Architect & DevOps Engineer**, we moved beyond ad-hoc analysis, implementing a star-schema data model and introducing **Power BI DevOps**. We treated our analysis like software development‚Äîversion-controlled, collaborative, and scalable.

**Then, we gave it a Voice.**
Data needs to be understood instantly. Our **UX/UI & Visualization Lead** directed the strategy to ensure the dashboard felt as premium as the products we were analyzing. Along with our **Visual Content Lead**, we segmented the vast Apple ecosystem into clear, actionable stories‚Äîfrom the dominance of iPhones to the attach rates of Apple Care for Wearables.

**Finally, we built an Ecosystem.**
We recognized that stakeholders consume data differently. While Power BI served as our central hub, we expanded our reach through **Tableau Integration** for cross-platform validation and **Spreadsheet Analytics** for granular reporting.

The result is not just a set of charts, but a fully integrated decision-support system that bridges the gap between raw transactions and strategic action.

Our goal was not merely to report revenue, but to construct a complete ecosystem that tracks the lifecycle of Apple products‚Äîfrom the moment a device is unboxed to how it is protected. By leveraging **Power BI DevOps**, rigorous **Data Modeling**, and **Multi-Platform Integration** (Tableau & Excel), we transformed raw rows into actionable strategies for every product category.

## üõ†Ô∏è The Architecture
The project is built upon a robust star-schema foundation, integrating two core datasets:
1.  **Sales Table:** The heartbeat of revenue, capturing product movement.
2.  **Warranty Table:** The post-purchase tracking, analyzing service attachment rates.
3.  **Products Table:** a created dim products table.
4.  **Running Date Table:** a created dim date table.

   **Desktop-Laptop Sales Table** [Out of the schema] a snapshot of **Sales Table** to make analysis on them easier.
